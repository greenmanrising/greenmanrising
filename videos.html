Videos

Bedlam Boys

Performed at the Tewkesbury Mediaeval Festival 2013. The words of this song come from the poem “Mad Maudlin’s Search for Her Tom of Bedlam” published in 1720 by Thomas d’Urfey in the book “Wit and Mirth, or Pills to Purge Melancholy”.

The ballad of Vic Williams

A song written by the excellent Robb Johnson. Video taken by “Joybert” at Godiva Festival 2010. Can’t see the embedded video below?

The Italian Set

For some reason this gets called “The Italian set”. The first tune is indeed Italian, a curenta of uncertain origins remembered by Andrew from a long lost street recording.

The second is an 18th century English tune called Miss Sayers Allemande, which can be found in the Fallibroome Collection.

Video taken by “Joybert” at Godiva Festival 2010

Ratcliffe Highway

A great 19th Century song of a sailor home on leave who has a narrow escape in one of the numerous seedy hostelries on the famous Ratcliffe Highway. Video taken by “Joybert” at Godiva Festival 2010.
